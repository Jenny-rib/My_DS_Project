🌍 Querying and Visualizing Air Pollution Data

This project utilizes SPARQL queries on Wikidata and Python-based visualization to explore structured data on air pollution. It examines the causes, impacts, research trends, and country-specific pollution records to provide a multi-perspective understanding of environmental challenges.


🛠️ Key Components(Main Analysis Topics):

  • Sources of air pollution (motor vehicles and wildfires)
  • Environmental and health impacts of air pollution
  • Research hotspots and keyword frequency (word cloud)
  • Country-level data comparison on pollution-related records
  • Annual trends in scholarly publications related to air pollution

🧰 Tools & Technologies：

  • Data Source: Wikidata linked open knowledge base
  • Query Language: SPARQL
  • Visualization: Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, Plotly

👩‍💻 My Work：

  • Designed and wrote multiple SPARQL queries to extract semantic data related to air pollution from Wikidata
  • Performed data cleaning, analysis, and visualization using Python
  • Created a word cloud to highlight frequently used keywords in academic titles
  • Analyzed publication year trends to track the evolution of air pollution research
  • Drafted the final report and presentation with my group.

🌟 Outcome：

  • Demonstrated how to analyze real-world environmental issues using Linked Open Data and semantic queries
  • Strengthened understanding of SPARQL, Wikidata, and knowledge graph structures
